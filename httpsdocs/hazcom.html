What is HazCom?

OSHA's Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200) ensures every worker exposed to hazardous chemicals knows what those chemicals are, what the risks are, and how to protect themselves.

Known as the "Right to Know" standard.

Aligned with the Globally Harmonized System (GHS) — a worldwide standard for classifying chemicals and communicating hazards through labels and Safety Data Sheets.

Fun fact: HazCom is consistently one of OSHA's Top 10 most-cited violations every year.

The Three Pillars of HazCom

Clark's written program must describe how each of these will be met (Section 5):

🏷️

Labeling

Every container of hazardous chemicals must be properly labeled with GHS elements

📄

Safety Data Sheets

SDS on-site for every hazardous chemical, maintained by the Project Safety Manager

🎓

Training

At initial assignment and whenever new chemical hazards are introduced

Remove any one pillar and the whole program collapses.

Reading a GHS Label

Per Section 6(a), every container must have these 6 elements. Click each one.

① Product Identifier
Chemical name or product number — links to SDS
② Signal Word
DANGER (severe) or WARNING (less severe)
③ Hazard Statements
Describes the nature and degree of hazard
④ Pictograms
Red diamond symbols indicating hazard class
⑤ Precautionary Statements
How to handle, store, and protect yourself
⑥ Manufacturer Info
Name, address, and telephone number

Signal Words

There are only two. Know the difference. A label will never have both.

DANGER

More severe hazards

Acute toxicity (fatal/toxic), explosives, flammable gases Cat 1

WARNING

Less severe hazards

Irritants, harmful if swallowed, combustible dusts

10

Employee Training

When?

  • At initial assignment
  • Whenever a new chemical hazard is introduced
  • Annual refresher (like right now!)

Training Must Cover:

  • How to detect chemical presence or release
  • Physical & health hazards in the work area
  • Protective measures — PPE, emergency procedures
  • How to read labels & use SDS

Non-routine tasks: Employees must also be informed of hazards from non-routine tasks and unlabeled pipes in their work areas.

⚠️

🏷️

Labeling System

Inform other employers of the labeling system used in the workplace

What's New for 2026

OSHA HazCom 2024 Final Rule — aligning with GHS Revision 7

New Hazard Classifications

Desensitized explosives and chemicals under pressure are now distinct categories. Aerosol reclassification is in effect.

Small Container Labeling

New flexibility for containers ≤100 mL — fold-out labels, tags, and pull-out labels are now permitted.

Updated Classification Criteria

Revised cut-off values for flammable gases, updated acute toxicity estimates, and new concentration limits for mixtures.

Compliance Timeline

Manufacturers & importers: Must update labels and SDS by July 2026. Employers: Workplace compliance by January 2027.

Key Takeaways

1. HazCom stands on 3 pillars: Labels, SDS, and Training — all three are required.

2. Every container must be labeled. Every chemical needs an SDS. Every employee needs training.

3. Know the 8 GHS pictograms and the two signal words (DANGER / WARNING).

4. 2026 updates are here — watch for new SDS formats, revised labels, and updated hazard classes.

5. As management, you are the enforcement layer. Set the standard and hold the line.
